Uniting Malayalee Community Through Culture Service

Founded in 1987, Malayalee Association of Greater Houston (MAGH) has led numerous successful campaigns that have impacted Malayalee Communities in Stafford, Missouri city, Sugar Land and across Houston. Through events, telethons, sports tournaments, and community programs organized by MAGH, we reach out to all the Malayalee communities in Houston and surrounding areas. MAGH conducts various Celebrations throughout the year such as Republic Day, Independence Day and other Indian festivals. ONAM is considered the most popular and the biggest event among MAGH members. MAGH also conducts many Community Events such as Blood Drives , Passport Fairs, Vaccination Campaigns, as well as several Seminars that focused to help our youths and adults. MAGH Charity has helped so many in the past years and currently helping 100 people who are not able to afford the cost of surgery in Kerala, India to get FREE Cataract Surgery.

Discover More

Malayalee Association of Greater Houston, Story

MAGH organized 6 Passport fairs in the last 3 years by Fort Bend County for residents in Houston and its suburbs. Conducted several sports tournaments every year for Basketball, Volleyball, Cricket, Badminton and Soccer for youngsters to promote unity, friendship, and fellowship in our community. We also conducted meetings and seminars about Career Guidance, Drug abuse awareness among our youths as well as importance of Voting, Tax and Medicare Laws, Retirement planning seminars for Adults. Moreover, we are also providing transportation for medical appointments for our senior members who are not able to drive. Since MAGH is a 501(c)3 corporation, all accounts are transparent and any donations made to us are tax exempt.
