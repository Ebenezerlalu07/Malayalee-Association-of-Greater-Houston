About Magh

Malayalee Association of Greater Houston, Story

Founded in 1987, Malayalee Association of Greater Houston (MAGH) has led numerous successful campaigns that have impacted Malayalee Communities in Stafford, Missouri city, Sugar Land and across Houston. Through events, telethons, sports tournaments, and community programs organized by MAGH, we reach out to all the Malayalee communities in Houston and surrounding areas. MAGH conducts various Celebrations throughout the year such as Republic Day, Independence Day and other Indian festivals. ONAM is considered the most popular and the biggest event among MAGH members. MAGH also conducts many Community Events such as Blood Drives , Passport Fairs, Vaccination Campaigns, as well as several Seminars that focused to help our youths and adults. MAGH Charity has helped so many in the past years and currently helping 100 people who are not able to afford the cost of surgery in Kerala, India to get FREE Cataract Surgery.


Volunteer Activities

As a non-profit organization, MAGH has conducted several charitable events to help the American community and also took part in charitable causes in other parts of the world during natural disasters and pandemics. Our volunteers joined the officials to arrange shelters, serving food and providing medical assistance for the victims of Hurricane Katrina in New Orleans. Our volunteers worked for weeks to clean and rebuild the houses and provided food/water to the residents of Missouri City and City of Houston during Hurricane Harvey.Our volunteers organized several food drives to collect food items and delivered them to the Houston Food Bank during the Covid-19 pandemic. In the past years, we organized several blood drives with MD Anderson Cancer Center and Gulf Coast Blood Bank.Teaming up with "Let Them Smile Again", an organization that belongs to the MAGH members, we were able to organize free medical camps in Columbia, Mexico and India, for children with cleft lip palate and done corrective surgery to fix them.Currently, we are planning to offer free Cataract surgery for one hundred people in Kerala, who are not able to afford the cost of surgery.


Volunteer Activities

MAGH organized 6 Passport fairs in the last 3 years by Fort Bend County for residents in Houston and its suburbs. Conducted several sports tournaments every year for Basketball, Volleyball, Cricket, Badminton and Soccer for youngsters to promote unity, friendship, and fellowship in our community. We also conducted meetings and seminars about Career Guidance, Drug abuse awareness among our youths as well as importance of Voting, Tax and Medicare Laws, Retirement planning seminars for Adults. Moreover, we are also providing transportation for medical appointments for our senior members who are not able to drive. Since MAGH is a 501(c)3 corporation, all accounts are transparent and any donations made to us are tax exempt.


Magh Vision

Our vision is to build a strong, united, and vibrant Malayalee community in Greater Houston by preserving and celebrating our rich cultural heritage while fostering a spirit of unity, service, and compassion. We strive to create meaningful opportunities for members of all ages to connect, grow, and support one another through cultural programs, community initiatives, and charitable activities.

Through active community engagement, volunteer service, and educational programs, we aim to inspire future generations to embrace their roots, develop leadership qualities, and contribute positively to society. By promoting togetherness, cultural pride, and social responsibility, we aspire to make a lasting impact within our community and extend our support to those in need both locally and globally.
